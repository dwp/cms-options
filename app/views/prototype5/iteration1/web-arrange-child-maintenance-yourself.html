Arrange child maintenance yourself

You can make your own maintenance arrangements between parents. No one else has to be involved and it can be whatever you agree. For example, you can:

  • decide how much one parent will pay
  • change the amount when needed
  • decide how often payments will be made
  • agree for a parent to pay for specific things, like school uniforms or trips

Making your own arrangement might not work if:

  • you don’t think the other parent will pay
  • you don’t feel safe dealing with the other parent

If making your own arrangement would not work you can use the Child Maintenance Service.

Working out payments

If you agree to make regular payments, you can use the child maintenance calculator to help agree an amount.

It shows you how much would be paid if you used the Child Maintenance Service, but if you're making your own arrangement you can negotiate something different.

Get help to make an arrangement

You can read guidance on:

  • working out the cost of raising your children
  • having a conversation about child maintenance
  • recording your agreement

You can contact Child Maintenance Options for support.
