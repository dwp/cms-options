I'm now going to tell you about your options

I have to tell you about both of your options, regardless of your personal circumstances. This is to make sure you have all the information you need to make the best decision for you and your children.

One option is to use the Child Maintenance Service

  • using the Child Maintenance Service is less flexible than making your own arrangement because the amount is based on the paying parent’s earnings
  • it might be a good option if you do not feel safe dealing with the other parent or you do not think they will stick to an agreement

You will need to complete an application online or on the phone

  • there is a £20 fee to make an application – you will not need to pay the fee if you are under 19 or if you have experienced any domestic abuse
  • the fee is non-refundable
  • it does not guarantee an arrangement

Then the Child Maintenance Service will:

  • work out how much child maintenance you will be paid
  • confirm the other parent's salary with HMRC to make sure your calculation is accurate
  • arrange for the other parent to pay you
  • help find the other parent if you've lost contact
  • recalculate the payment amount if changes to either parents’ circumstances are reported. For example, if the other parent's salary changes
  • help if someone says they are not the parent of the child
  • take action if payments are not made

Do you have any questions about any of that?


Most parents who use the Child Maintenance Service will start with a 'Direct Pay' arrangement

This means:

  • the other parent will pay maintenance directly to your bank account
  • you do not need to have any contact with the other parent
  • other than the application fee, there are no additional fees if you use Direct Pay

If you do not think Direct Pay will work, you might be able to use Collect and Pay:

  • you can only start with Collect and Pay if the other parent agrees to it or if they do not respond to us
  • the Child Maintenance Service will collect maintenance from the other parent and pass it on to you

There are extra fees for Collect and Pay, which are:

  • 20% will be added to the other parent's payment
  • 4% will be taken off the payment you receive

For example, if the payment amount was £100, you would get £96 and the other parent would pay £120

Do not worry if you did not take all that in. We'll tell you more about the payment types if you choose to use the Child Maintenance Service.


Do you have any questions about Direct Pay or Collect and Pay?


Your other option is to make your own arrangement

You do not need to go through this information if the caller states they have already tried a private arrangement or that it will not work for them. You can ask if they have any questions about making a private arrangement and move to the next section.
  • this is a private arrangement between you and the other parent
  • no one else has to be involved
  • it can be whatever you and the other parent agree to

You and the other parent can:

  • come to an agreement about how much you will be paid
  • change the amount if you need to
  • decide how often you will be paid
  • agree for the other parent to pay for specific things, like school uniforms or trips

Making your own arrangement might be a good option if:

  • you feel safe dealing with the other parent
  • you think the other parent will stick to an agreement
  • you and the other parent can communicate well

Do you have any questions about any of that?


If you choose to make a private arrangement, there is support available online.

For example:

  • you can work out the cost of raising your children
  • a guide you can follow when talking about child maintenance with the other parent
  • guidance on how you can record your agreement

You can find support online by searching 'making a child maintenance arrangement'.


Do you have any questions about the support that is available for private arrangements?
